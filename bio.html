Bio

I received B.S. and M.S. degrees in Applied Mathematics from the University of Colorado at Boulder, where I developed novel stochastic weather generators under the supervision of Will Kleiber. I moved to Seattle in 2016 to pursue a Ph.D. in Statistics at the University of Washington in Seattle.

Currently, I am a predoctoral research associate for the Matsen Group in the Computational Biology Program at the Fred Hutchinson Cancer Research Center working on statistical and computational methods for B and T Cell receptor sequence analysis.

I have internship experience as a data scientist for Paysa, analytics developer for Seagate, and software engineer for Spectra.
